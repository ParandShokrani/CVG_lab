Yvonne Fahy

Yvonne Fahy is a doctoral researcher with a keen interest in cell biology and biomedical research. She holds an Honours Bachelor’s degree in General Science, specialising in Anatomy, as well as an Honours Master’s degree in Regenerative Medicine. These combined experiences have given her a deep understanding and appreciation for how the human body functions on a macroscopic and microscopic level. She is excited to be undertaking a PhD through the School of Medicine as part of the TrapKill Project, which focuses on developing innovative treatments for glioblastoma using functionalised hydrogels. Yvonne is inspired by the possibility of bridging research with real-world impact and by the potential of these solutions to improve patient outcomes. She hopes to contribute to the development of advanced, patient-focused therapies and to play an active role in translating scientific discoveries into clinical practice.
